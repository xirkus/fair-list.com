It's All About the Agents

{{ page.excerpt }}

You should keep this in mind when looking for, and listing FSBO properties on Zillow. While Zillow is a great resource for getting comprehensive information about listings and the local market, access to this information comes at a price.

The Real Cost

By Zillow's own account on FSBO listings, approximately 36% of sellers attempt to sell their homes themselves. Only 11% are successful. These numbers clearly show the desire in the marketplace for FSBO. Unfortunately, the system is designed to work against you. How does Zillow undermine FSBO?

  • As a seller, there is no way to opt out of the Premier Agent Program. You can't even pay Zillow to do so.

  • Conversely, as a buyer, Zillow's Premier Agent program will "suggest" agents or realtors who may not actually represent the seller. This is highly misleading and is designed to introduce a buyer's agent into the process.

  • FSBO is completely missing from their mobile application. The mobile app is designed to funnel your listings to agents. There is no stronger indication of Zillow's priorities by completely excluding this feature from its applications.

  • Zillow's only concern is for the agents. The only reason Zillow lists FSBO is to convert these leads to for sale by agent (FSBA). Still unsure? Checkout this article for their Premier Agent program regarding FSBO listings.

    There's even a term in the industry for this type of lead generation - "prospecting". When an agent asks to "preview" your property, what they're doing is pretending to have an interested buyer when all they want is to convert your listing to FSBA.

Caveat Emptor

By all means advertise your property on Zillow. After all, it's free. But be aware of the challenges you're likely to encounter as both a buyer and seller of FSBO properties. This is not surprising given agents are Zillow's primary customer. Instead, buyers and sellers are being used either for the listing information they contribute to the market, or for the traffic they generate through search.

This is the cost of "free".
