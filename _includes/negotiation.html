Negotiating – Keeping a Level Head

The best negotiators are empathetic, meaning they can see another person’s perspective. That does not mean that they are emotional. While negotiation can be seen as a “winner-take-all” challenge, it is more productive to approach it as reaching a beneficial consensus where both parties win by getting what’s important to them. This is especially true for a successful real estate transaction.

Successful negotiations require:

  1. Listening. Without listening, you can’t know what’s important to the other person.
  2. Understanding what you consider non-negotiable and communicating that clearly. This also means being prepared to walk away.
  3. Addressing the other person's concerns. This may require both sides to reciprocate with something negotiable.
  4. Being patient. Rushing to a conclusion is not the goal; a mutually beneficial outcome is.
  5. Remembering to be empathetic, but not emotional. Don’t take it personally.
